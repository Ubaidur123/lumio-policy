# Privacy Policy for Lumio

**Last updated:** [Insert Date]

Lumio ("we", "our", "the app") is a communication support app designed to help autistic children express themselves, intended for use by children under the guidance of a parent or caregiver. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding that information.

## Information We Collect

**Account Information**
When a parent or caregiver creates an account to use Lumio, we collect:
- Email address
- Password (securely stored and encrypted; we do not have access to your plain-text password)

We do not collect the child's name, date of birth, photo, or any other personally identifying information about the child using the app.

**Data We Do Not Collect**
Lumio does not collect location data, contacts, device identifiers for advertising, or browsing history. Lumio does not display ads and does not share data with advertising networks.

## How We Use Information

We use the account email and password solely to:
- Authenticate your account and allow secure sign-in
- Save and sync your app settings/preferences across sessions

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

## Third-Party Services

Lumio uses **Firebase** (a service provided by Google) for account authentication and data storage. Firebase may process account information (email) as part of providing these services. Firebase's own privacy practices are governed by Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

Lumio uses on-device text-to-speech technology to support communication features. Text entered or selected within the app for speech output is processed to generate audio and is not stored or transmitted beyond what is necessary for the app to function.

## Children's Privacy

Lumio is designed for use by children under the direct supervision of a parent or caregiver, who is responsible for creating and managing the account. We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children. The account holder (parent/caregiver) is the individual who provides their email address during sign-up.
